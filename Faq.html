Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life, with unique characteristics that make it a fascinating subject of study.
General Facts About Earth
Position in the Solar System: Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the largest of the four terrestrial planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars). 
2
Formation: Earth formed approximately 4.54 billion years ago and is the only planet known to support life. 
1
Atmosphere and Water: Earth has a unique atmosphere that allows for the presence of liquid water, which is essential for life. It is the only planet where water exists in all three states: solid, liquid, and gas. 
2
Rotation and Orbit: Earth completes one rotation on its axis approximately every 24 hours and takes about 365.25 days to orbit the Sun. This axial tilt of 23.4 degrees is responsible for the changing seasons. 
2
Population: As of now, Earth is home to over 8 billion people, with India and China being the most populated countries.

What is Earth's "Dark Age"?: This refers to the first 500 million years after Earth's formation, a period about which little is known due to the scarcity of preserved rocks from that time.

Why does Earth have plate tectonics?: Researchers are investigating the reasons behind Earth's tectonic activity and its relationship with the presence of water and life.
